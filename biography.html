Master Manzoor

Master Manzoor

Master Manzoor’s real name was "Qader Hussain Wadho". He belonged to the "Qambar-Shahdadkot" district. His father, "Makhno Faqir", was a well-known classical and folk singer. From an early age, Manzoor developed a deep interest in music.
He received his education in "Dadu" and continued his studies up to the university level. Later, he decided to pursue music as his full-time career.
Master Manzoor became one of the most popular Sindhi singers. His famous song "Harjae Pireen, Har Mehfil Main Mukhe Tuhenji Kami Mehsoos Thi They" is considered a classic in Sindhi music.
He released several successful albums, including "Sacho Ishq", "Naseeb", "Dilber Ayo", "Sehra", and "Tuhenjoon Yadoon". His voice was known for being emotional, soft, and powerful.
He passed away on "April 22, 2012", at the age of around 42. Initially, his death was reported as suicide, but later the police also investigated it as a possible murder.
Master Manzoor modernized Sindhi folk music and introduced a new style that connected deeply with the youth. Even today, his songs remain popular and are remembered with great respect.

Manzoor Sakhirani

Manzoor Sakhirani

Manzoor Sakhirani’s real name is "Manzoor Ali". He was born on "April 14, 1959", in "District Dadu, Sindh". He is one of the most popular and respected Sindhi singers who gave Sindhi music a new identity and earned a special place in the hearts of his people.
His father, "Baharuddin", worked in the Revenue Department. He wanted his son to become an engineer, but from childhood, Manzoor had a deep love for music. He completed his primary and intermediate education in "Dadu", then joined an engineering university. During his studies, he realized that music was his true passion.
He learned classical music from "Ustad Qudrat Ullah", who taught him the depth of "sur", "taal", and "raag". Under his guidance, Manzoor refined his voice, tone, and musical expression, developing a unique style that blended emotion and melody.
In "1980", he began his professional music career. His first album, "Band Darwazo Ta", was released the same year and became a major hit across Sindh. After that, he released over "30 albums", each with a different theme and musical flavor. His songs often reflect Sindhi culture, folk tales, and deep human emotions.

Some of his most loved songs include:

* Band Darwazo Ta
* Mohinja Maran Ta Kad Nahi Aeen
* Tunjo Pyar Mitha Lagando Aa
* Dil Lagi
* Aa Dilbar Aa
* Tuhinji Yaad Mein

His songs are not just for entertainment; they express the emotions, pain, and love found in Sindhi life. Through his voice, he captured the cultural spirit of his people.
Manzoor’s voice carries the soul of Sindhi tradition. He has performed in many cultural festivals, television programs, and gatherings across Pakistan. People affectionately call him "Ustad Manzoor Sakhirani", a title that shows their respect for his artistry. His singing style is simple, heartfelt, and filled with emotion.
Over the years, he brought Sindhi music to a new generation, helping the youth connect with their language and culture. His performances turned him into a cultural icon across Sindh.
In "2018", the "Dadu Press Club" celebrated his "59th birthday" and honored his lifelong contribution to Sindhi music.
Even today, his songs are played in homes, on radios, and in public gatherings. His voice remains alive in the hearts of his fans, and his legacy continues as a symbol of Sindhi pride and culture

Fozia Somroo

Fozia Somroo

Fozia Soomro’s real name was "Hakeema". She was born in "1966" in "Kharsar, Tharparkar", Sindh. She came from a simple, religious, and culturally rich family. Her father, "Haji Nathoo Soomro", was a "Maulood" and "Qawwali" singer who helped her recognize her natural talent and voice from childhood.
When Fozia was young, her family faced financial hardships. Because of this, they moved from Tharparkar to "Tando Muhammad Khan", where she began a new chapter of her life.
From an early age, she had a strong passion for music. At that time, however, it was not considered acceptable for girls to sing publicly. Despite these social restrictions, she began singing secretly at women’s gatherings and small events. By the age of sixteen, she had already won people’s hearts with her natural, emotional, and melodious voice.
Her true recognition came when "Naseer Mirza", a producer at "Radio Pakistan Hyderabad", heard her sing. He recorded two songs with her, "Be Qadra Qadr Na Kayaee Ko" and "Chade Wai Chade Wai Sathi Sukhan Ja". Both songs became extremely popular across Sindh, turning Fozia Soomro into a star overnight.
Throughout her career, she sang in "Sindhi", "Thari", and "Marwari" languages. Her style was powerful, emotional, and deeply connected to Sindhi folk music traditions. Her songs expressed the pain, love, separation, and emotions of women.
She often performed at "Sufi" and cultural events. During the 1990s, her songs were played in every cassette shop and on every radio station across Sindh. She released "over 50 albums and cassettes", and her voice reached both urban and rural areas.
Some of her most famous songs include:
* Be Qadra Qadr Na Kayaee Ko
* Chade Wai Chade Wai Sathi Sukhan Ja
* To Kehri Ibaadat Aa Jo Manjho Dil Na Mane
* Mitho Maro Mohan
* Na Ja Dilber Na Ja
* Suhni Saheli

Fozia Soomro gave Sindhi folk music a new identity. At a time when the Sindhi music industry was dominated by male singers, she used her voice to open the door for women. Her songs spread to every corner of Sindh, especially in "Tharparkar" and "Badin" regions.
During her career, she received the "Shah Abdul Latif Bhitai Award" and several other regional honors. For the people of Sindh, her music was more than just entertainment. It became a symbol of Sindhi identity and culture.
Fozia Soomro’s life was short, but her art lived long. She passed away on "4 September 2002" due to "kidney failure" at the age of only "36". After her death, the people of Sindh and local media gave her the title "Bemisaal Thari Rani" (The Unmatched Queen of Thar).
Even today, her songs are played on radio, YouTube, and at cultural gatherings. Her voice carries the essence of Sindh—its deserts, traditions, and spirit. She gave Sindhi and Marwari music a unique identity and earned a permanent place in the hearts of Sindhi people.
Her name still stands among the legends of Sindhi music, and her voice remains one of the most beautiful memories of Sindh.

ghulam shabbir samo

ghulam shabbir samo

Ghulam Shabbir Samo is a popular "Sindhi folk and Sufi singer" known for his soulful voice and deep connection with Sindhi cultural traditions. He was born in "Sindh, Pakistan", and grew up in an environment rich in local music, poetry, and Sufi influence. From a young age, he was drawn to the sounds of "Sindhi folk music, which became the foundation of his singing career.
Ghulam Shabbir started performing at "local melas and cultural events", where his emotional voice quickly gained recognition. His songs often carry strong themes of "love, spirituality, and devotion", reflecting the poetic spirit of Sindh. Over time, his name became associated with modern Sindhi folk revival.
He gained popularity through his performances on "Sindhi television programs" and "Radio Pakistan", as well as on "digital platforms" like YouTube and Apple Music. His music blends traditional Sindhi melodies with modern arrangements, keeping the essence of the region’s heritage alive while appealing to younger listeners.
Some of his famous works include:

* 31 Sik Bharyo Salam
* Mushkil Kusha Ali Chau
* Sindhi

His songs are known for their heartfelt lyrics, composed in "Sindhi and Saraiki dialects", and for their use of traditional instruments such as the "dholak", "ektara", and "algoza".
Ghulam Shabbir Samo represents a new generation of Sindhi singers who continue to preserve "Sufi poetry and folk heritage" through their voice. His dedication to his craft has made him one of the respected names in Sindhi music today.
He continues to perform across Sindh and other parts of Pakistan, spreading messages of peace, unity, and spirituality through his songs.

Mumtaz Lashari

Mumtaz Lashari

Mumtaz Lashari is a popular Pakistani singer known for his deep and soulful voice. He was born in Sindh, Pakistan, and became one of the most respected names in Sindhi and Siraiki music. From a young age, he showed a strong passion for singing and poetry. He grew up listening to traditional Sindhi folk songs and classical tunes, which shaped his unique musical style.
Mumtaz Lashari started his career by performing at local cultural events and Sindhi television programs. His talent was soon recognized by music producers who helped him record his first few songs. His early songs became widely popular and established him as a leading voice in regional music.
He is known for blending "traditional Sindhi melodies" with "modern musical instruments" to create a rich and emotional sound. His songs often reflect the beauty of Sindhi culture, love, pain, and social themes. Because of his unique voice and powerful lyrics, he gained a large fan base not only in Sindh but across Pakistan and among overseas Sindhi communities.
Some of his most famous songs include "Tuhinji Kawar Mein Qurb", "Pakhi Piyar Jo", and "Naina Moray". These songs show his range, emotional depth, and mastery over folk rhythms. His music has been released on popular platforms such as YouTube, Apple Music, and SoundCloud.
In recognition of his contributions to Sindhi and Pakistani music, Mumtaz Lashari received the "Pride of Performance" award from the Government of Pakistan in "March 2015". This award highlights his dedication and lifelong service to promoting Sindhi culture and music.
Mumtaz Lashari continues to perform in concerts and cultural events across the country. He also supports young artists and encourages them to keep Sindhi folk music alive. His legacy is defined by his commitment to preserving traditional sounds while making them meaningful for modern audiences.

Sadiq Faqeer

Sadiq Faqeer

Sadiq Faqeer was a renowned Sindhi folk and Sufi singer from Pakistan. He was born in Mithi, Tharparkar, Sindh, in 1967. He belonged to a respected family known for its devotion to Sindhi culture and literature. From childhood, he was drawn to poetry and music, especially the verses of Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai and other Sufi poets.
He began his early education in Mithi and later graduated from Sindh University. During his student life, he developed his singing skills under the guidance of local ustads who taught him classical and folk techniques. His pure, soulful voice and deep understanding of Sufi poetry made him one of the most respected singers in Sindhi music.
Sadiq Faqeer started his career by performing at cultural programs and radio shows. His first breakthrough came from “Radio Pakistan Hyderabad,” where his songs gained massive popularity. His famous renditions include “Kechh Aahe Karoon Huzoor,” “Jeay Sindh Jeay Latif,” and “Aa Dilbar Aa.” His singing reflected messages of peace, love, and spirituality.
He was known for his command over both folk and classical styles, performing poetry from Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai, Sachal Sarmast, and Shaikh Ayaz with devotion and emotion. His songs carried Sindh’s cultural depth and the spiritual message of harmony and tolerance.
Sadiq Faqeer performed across Pakistan and internationally, representing Sindhi culture at music festivals in India, UAE, and Europe. He received multiple awards for his services to Sindhi music, including “Shah Latif Award” and “Sufi Excellence Award.”
Tragically, on February 26, 2015, Sadiq Faqeer passed away in a car accident near Umerkot. His death was a great loss to Sindhi music and culture.
Even today, his songs continue to inspire love, peace, and Sindhi identity. His name remains among the most respected legends of Sindhi folk and Sufi music.

Sarmad Sindhi

Sarmad Sindhi

Sarmad Sindhi’s real name was “Abdul Rehman Mughal”. He was born on “7 July 1961” in “Piryaloi, Khairpur District, Sindh, Pakistan”. He emerged as a folk singer who used the Sindhi language and is considered one of the notable voices of the golden era of Sindhi literature and music.
Sarmad Sindhi began his musical career around the year “1978” and remained active until his untimely death in “1996”. His songs covered a wide range of folk styles, and he also used his lyrics to speak about the issues of the people of Sindh—for example the line “Sindh uchi aa, Sindhi uchi aa” (“Sindh is high, Sindhi is high”).
Among his most popular songs were “Tuhiji Yaad Ji Wari Aa Weer” and “Piyar Manjharan Pengho Loday Loli Diyan”, which became generational anthems across Sindh.
Tragically, Sarmad Sindhi died on “27 December 1996” in a road accident while travelling from Badin to Karachi. His death left a significant void in the Sindhi music world and his legacy continues in his songs and social work.
Today, Sarmad Sindhi is still remembered not only as a great singer but as someone whose voice expressed the culture, struggles and pride of Sindh.

Shaman Ali Mirali

Shaman Ali Mirali

Shaman Ali Mirali was born on “1 January 1971” in a village near “Shikarpur”, Sindh, Pakistan. His father was “Lal Baksh Mirali”, who also sang Sufi songs and naats, which influenced young Shaman’s musical journey.
From early childhood, Shaman showed interest in singing and performing. He started singing publicly at local events and “Urs of Budhal Faqeer” where his voice gained recognition. He received formal music training under “Ustad Ghulam Haider” in Jacobabad, where he stayed for nearly five years to refine his craft.
Mirali’s career spans Sindhi folk, Sufi, spiritual and modern styles. He has released many popular tracks, such as “Dilan Ja Malik” and “Sohnri Sindhi Jiye”.
He has performed internationally, including a noted appearance in Dubai in 2018 for a Sindhi cultural show organized by the Mehran Cultural Council alongside other Sindhi stars.
Shaman Ali Mirali is respected for keeping Sindhi folk heritage alive while appealing to modern audiences. His command over both traditional and contemporary music styles makes him a key figure in Sindhi music today.

Ahmed Mughal

Ahmed Mughal

"Ahmed Mughal" is a well-known Sindhi folk singer from "Piryaloi", a small town in "Khairpur Mir’s District" (Sindh, Pakistan). He received his early education from "Government High School Piryaloi" and later studied at "Government Degree College Sukkur".
He began his musical career around 1998 when he got his first opportunity to perform on television. His debut album was also released the same year. Over time, he gained wide popularity in Sindhi music circles and has released around 37 albums with more than 300 recorded songs.
Ahmed Mughal has collaborated with major Sindhi television and production channels such as "KTN", "Sindh TV", and "Awaz TV". His famous songs include "Jiye Sindh Jiye 2.0" and "Sindhi Jity Bhi Hujan".
He is known for promoting Sindhi culture and language through his music. His songs reflect the traditions, pride, and cultural beauty of Sindh. Ahmed Mughal’s voice and compositions continue to inspire the younger generation, making him one of the prominent figures in Sindhi folk music.
Though information about his personal life such as date of birth or family background is not widely available, his contribution to Sindhi music is significant and well-respected.

Rajab Faqeer

Rajab Faqeer

"Rajab Faqeer" is a Sindhi classical and folk singer from "Thar, Sindh, Pakistan". He has earned recognition for his soulful performances and cultural representation of Sindhi folk traditions.
He is associated with the "Murad Faqeer Academy Mithi", where he performs and teaches music. Rajab Faqeer’s presence on social media, especially on YouTube and Facebook, has helped spread Sindhi music to a wider audience.
He has performed songs in multiple languages including Sindhi, Saraiki, Pashto, and Urdu, focusing mostly on folk, Sufi, and poetic themes. Some of his live performances, such as "Jawani Na Rehndi", have become very popular among local and international audiences.
Rajab Faqeer is appreciated for bringing the traditional music of Thar to the mainstream, giving voice to regional heritage. His unique tone, emotional delivery, and spiritual style have made him one of the respected artists in the Sindhi music scene.
Although detailed information about his birth, education, and personal background is limited, his artistic work continues to play a vital role in preserving and promoting Sindhi musical culture.
